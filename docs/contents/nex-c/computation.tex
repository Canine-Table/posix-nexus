\begin{NxSBox}[breakable][Computational Theory]
	\begin{NxIDBox}
	Computational Theory explores the fundamental principles that govern computation, including models of computation, complexity classes, and the limits of algorithmic processing.
	\end{NxIDBox}
	\begin{NxIDBoxL}
		%\nxTopicD{Introduction to Computational Theory} Overview of key concepts, including formal computation models, problem-solving frameworks, and theoretical boundaries.
		%\nxTopicD{Turing Machines} The foundation of modern computation, introducing \textbf{deterministic} and \textbf{non-deterministic} Turing machines, decidability, and universal computation.
		\nxTopicD{Automata Theory} Examines formal models like\textbf{finite-state automata (\nxGID{dfa}, \nxGID{nfa})} and\textbf{context-free grammars}, essential for language processing and machine behavior.
		%\nxTopicD{Complexity Theory} Defines the\textbf{difficulty of problems}, categorizing them into \textbf{P, NP, NP-complete}, and \textbf{PSPACE} classes, shaping algorithm design and computational feasibility.
		%\nxTopicD{Computability Theory} Investigates\textbf{decidable vs. undecidable problems}, Gödel’s incompleteness theorem, and the \textbf{Halting Problem}, establishing the theoretical limits of computation.
	\end{NxIDBoxL}
\end{NxSBox}
